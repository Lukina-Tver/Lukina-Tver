# The Song of the Tver Region: An Anthem as a Civil Prayer

> *"An anthem is a solemn song... A civil prayer of the people."* — Olga Lukina, PhD.

---

## I. Two Anthems: Official vs. Public Initiative

The City of Tver currently possesses an official anthem written by the renowned poet **Andrey Dementiyev** and performed by **Joseph Kobzon**. 

### The Official Anthem of Tver City (by A. Dementiyev)
*Poetic Translation:*

City of courage, of honor and glory,  
Golden star shining through days of our own.  
Rightly named Tver, as told in your story,  
For like a fortress, your spirit is grown.  

**Chorus:** O, Land of Tver, forever be young,  
May sorrows and hardships ne'er touch your gate.  
To you our devotion and labors are flung,  
With you through the ages we link our own fate.

---

## II. Analysis: Pathos vs. Historical Depth

While Dementiyev’s text is a "stylistic model" and Joseph Kobzon’s performance was undeniably dignified, a true **ANTHEM** requires an epic scale. 

An anthem, as a genre, should feature:
* A clear, majestic structure.
* Laudatory comparisons and descriptions.
* A chronicle of hardships and feats endured by ancestors.

In the official version of the city anthem, epic hints are absent. There is no mention of the great figures such as **Mikhail of Tver**, **Anna of Kashin**, or **Afanasy Nikitin**. It lacks the "civil prayer" quality that unites a people through their shared history of labor and sacrifice. The City Duma's approval of this version often followed the "general party line" rather than deep verbal context.

## III. The Public Initiative: "The Song of the Tver Region"

In response to this, a public initiative emerged. Composers V. Uspensky, A. Venislavichev, and A. Shamkin approached our studio to create a visual sequence for a new work. 

The content of this new "Song" aligns with the true canons of hymnology, featuring the heroes of the past and the workers of the present. Its power was truly realized when it was performed by the **Male Choir of the Saint Danilov Monastery**.

### The Song of the Tver Region
*(Updated version with edits by Tver poet Ivan Demidov)*

Along beautiful Volga, keeping watch o'er our peace,  
Lies our ancient Tver land, may its power increase.  
Glorious deeds of our fathers, and the toil of their hands,  
Valor, Faith, and Devotion lead us through these lands.  

**Chorus:** Flourish now, our beloved, fatherland we hold dear!  
Glorify Great Russia! We are proud you are here!  

Your protector so holy – valiant Prince Mikhail,  
Gave his life for your sake – kept your honor's bright trail.  
Afanasy Nikitin gained a world-renowned name,  
As a merchant-enlightener – Eastward kindled his fame.  

**(Chorus)**

Conquering fire and hunger, the war, and the frost,  
You have fought every foe, though the battle had cost.  
O, our Tver land beloved, father’s house we adore,  
Kept by God’s holy faith, we are yours evermore.

**(Chorus)**

### Honoring the Legacy of A. V. Alexandrov
This project is inseparable from the efforts of **Andrey Venislavichev**, who works tirelessly to honor **Alexander Alexandrov** (the creator of the Russian Anthem's music) in Tver. Alexandrov served as a choirmaster in Tver for 13 years and remains a pinnacle of Russian hymnology.
